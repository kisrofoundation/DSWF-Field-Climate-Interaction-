## 📄 Description
The **Dilaso Spiral Wave Force (DSWF)** is proposed as a weak nonlinear wave field that introduces a secondary interaction layer within Earth's atmospheric system. This model explores how DSWF interacts with localized ionized regions formed due to solar radiation, cosmic rays, and atmospheric electrical activity.

Unlike conventional forces, DSWF does not create net electric charge but couples with existing ionization to produce subtle energy modulations. These interactions may contribute to small-scale atmospheric pressure fluctuations and influence climate energy balance through weak nonlinear dynamics.

This repository presents a structured theoretical framework under the broader **Beyond Gravity** concept, where gravity governs large-scale structure while DSWF contributes to fine-scale dynamical modulation.

## 🧪 Abstract
This work introduces the Dilaso Spiral Wave Force (DSWF) as a weak nonlinear wave field interacting with ionized atmospheric regions. The model preserves charge neutrality and conservation laws while introducing a secondary energy modulation mechanism.

DSWF is formulated using a nonlinear wave equation coupled with atmospheric density and ionization current terms. The framework predicts small contributions to atmospheric pressure and climate energy balance.

A unified interpretation termed **Beyond Gravity** is proposed, where gravitational structure is complemented by wave-based modulation. The model remains speculative but mathematically structured and suitable for further theoretical and experimental exploration.
